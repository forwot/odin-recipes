Katong Laksa

Rich, Spicy, Creamy, Umami - all in one bowl.

This is the recipe you’ve been waiting for, where everything is made from scratch, with love, from the paste to the broth to the sambal. It may look like a lot to take in, but none of the steps themselves are tricky once you get down to it – it just takes a while. Settle in for the afternoon and enjoy a spot of cooking therapy. It’s worth it.

Ingredients

  • 12 large raw king prawns (about 600g/1 lb 5 oz), peeled and cleaned (reserve shells and heads for stock)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 750ml (3 cups) coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp grated palm sugar
  • ¼ cup fish sauce, or to taste
  • 3 tbsp salt
  • 600g (1 lb 5 oz) dried round rice noodles – laksa or rice vermicelli noodles
  • 18 store-bought fish balls
  • 200g (7 oz) beanshoots
  • 150g (5 oz) fried tofu puffs
  • 300g (10.5 oz) blood cockles or clams, cooked, meat picked
  • finely sliced Vietnamese mint leaves, to serve

Spice Paste

  • 2 tbsp dried shrimp
  • 16 large dried red chillies
  • 5-6 small dried red chillies
  • 2 tsp belacan (Malaysian shrimp paste)
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 5-6 red Asian shallots, coarsely chopped
  • 1 tbsp chopped galangal
  • 1 tbsp chopped ginger
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 3 lemongrass stalks, trimmed, bruised, finely sliced
  • 8 macadamia nuts or candlenuts, coarsely chopped
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric

Stock

  • 1½ tbsp vegetable oil
  • reserved shells and heads from the prawns

Chilli Sambal

  • 1 cup large dried red chilies
  • 1 tbsp dried shrimp
  • 2 tsp belacan (Malaysian shrimp paste)
  • 5-6 fresh long red chillies
  • 3-4 Asian red shallots, coarsely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, coarsely chopped
  • 2 tsp, firmly packed, shaved palm sugar
  • 60ml (¼ cup) vegetable oil

Steps to Cook

Prepare the Spice Paste

  1. Cover the dried chillies with boiling water and soak for 30 minutes until softened. In a separate bowl, soak the dried shrimp in hot water for 20 minutes; drain, squeeze out excess water, and roughly chop.
  2. Once the chillies are soft, squeeze out the water (reserve the liquid), roughly chop, and add to a blender with the shrimp.
  3. Wrap the belacan in foil and dry-fry in a heavy-based pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes per side until fragrant. Add to the blender.
  4. Dry-roast coriander seeds in a pan for 3 minutes until fragrant, then grind into a fine powder. Add to the blender along with shallots, galangal, ginger, garlic, lemongrass, and macadamias.
  5. Process the mixture into a paste, adding a little reserved chilli-soaking liquid if needed. Stir in the turmeric by hand to avoid staining the blender.

Make the Chilli Sambal

  1. Soak dried chillies and dried shrimp in separate bowls of boiling water for 30 minutes. Dry-fry foil-wrapped belacan for 2-3 minutes per side.
  2. Drain and de-seed both the soaked and fresh chillies, then roughly chop. Drain and chop the dried shrimp.
  3. Place chillies, shrimp, belacan, shallot, garlic, palm sugar, 1½ tbsp water, and 1 tbsp oil in a food processor and blend until smooth.
  4. Heat the remaining oil in a wok over low-medium heat. Cook the paste, stirring constantly, for about 15 minutes or until the oil separates. Set aside.

Prepare the Prawn Stock

  1. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook prawn heads and shells for 10 minutes until they smell toasty and liquid has evaporated.
  2. Add 2 litres of water and bring to a simmer. Cook for 40 minutes, skimming any scum from the surface.
  3. Strain the stock, pressing the solids to extract flavor. Measure out 1.25 litres (top up with water if necessary).

Assemble the Laksa

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the noodles. In a separate large saucepan, heat oil and fry the spice paste for 4-5 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Stir in the coconut milk and prawn stock. Add palm sugar, fish sauce, and salt. Simmer gently over low heat for 15 minutes.
  3. Cook the noodles in the boiling water until tender. Drain, snip into short lengths with scissors, and divide into serving bowls.
  4. Using the noodle water, blanch the prawns for 4 minutes until cooked, then blanch the fish balls, beanshoots, and tofu puffs until warm. Add all to the bowls.
  5. Divide the cockles or clams among the bowls and ladle the hot laksa soup over the top.
  6. Garnish with Vietnamese mint and a dollop of chilli sambal. Serve immediately.
